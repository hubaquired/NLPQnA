Bulgarian Potatoes recipe.  Put oven rack in middle position and preheat oven to 375°F. Butter baking dish. Purée cottage cheese in a food processor until as smooth as possible, about 1 minute, then add butter, 1 teaspoon salt, and 1/2 teaspoon pepper and blend until combined well. Peel potatoes and cut crosswise using slicer into 1/8-inch-thick slices. Spread one third of potatoes evenly in 1 layer in dish and top with one third a scant cup) cottage cheese mixture mixture will not cover potatoes completely). Continue layering potatoes and cottage cheese mixture, ending with mixture. Cover tightly with foil and bake until potatoes are tender, 1 to 1 1/4 hours. Whisk together eggs, yogurt, remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt, and remaining 1/4 teaspoon pepper in a bowl. Pour egg mixture evenly over potatoes and bake, uncovered, until custard is set, about 20 minutes. Bulgarian Potatoes has 262.0 calories. Bulgarian Potatoes has 10.0 grams of protein. Bulgarian Potatoes has 13.0 grams of fat. Bulgarian Potatoes has 487.0 grams of sodium. The ingredients of Bulgarian Potatoes are 2 cups 4% cottage cheese (1 lb), 1 stick (1/2 cup) unsalted butter, melted and cooled slightly, 1 1/4 teaspoons salt, 3/4 teaspoon black pepper, 3 lb russet (baking) potatoes (6 medium), 2 large eggs, 1 cup plain whole-milk yogurt (8 oz), Special equipment: an adjustable-blade slicer; a 2 1/2-qt baking dish (about 12 by 10 by 2 inches).We weren't sure what would result from pouring an egg-and-yogurt mixture over a gratin of potatoes. The answer? A thin layer of tangy custard that just melts into the gratin. Like a much more sumptuous version of the sour-cream-topped baked potato, this dish is a welcome change of pace.
