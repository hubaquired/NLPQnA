Kabocha Squash Pilaf With Coconut recipe.  Combine currants and vinegar in a small bowl; set aside. Heat oil in a large skillet over medium; cook onion and ginger, stirring occasionally, until softened, 8–10 minutes. Stir in squash, rice, and 1 1/4 cups water; season with salt and pepper. Bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer until rice is tender, 15–18 minutes. Remove from heat, fluff rice with a fork, and let sit covered 10 minutes. Drain currants and serve pilaf topped with drained currants and toasted unsweetened coconut flakes. The ingredients of Kabocha Squash Pilaf With Coconut are 2 tablespoons dried currants, 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar, 2 tablespoons virgin coconut oil, 1 small onion, chopped, 4 thin slices peeled ginger, 1 cup shredded peeled kabocha squash, 1 cup basmati rice, rinsed, Kosher salt, freshly ground pepper, Toasted unsweetened coconut flakes (for serving).When you peel the kabocha squash for this recipe, use a vegetable peeler—not a knife.
