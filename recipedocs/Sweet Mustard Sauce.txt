Sweet Mustard Sauce recipe.  Combine the sweet mustard and 2 tablespoons of the Dijon mustard in a medium bowl. Add the sugar and 1 tablespoon of the vinegar. Gradually add the oil, whisking constantly. Adjust the flavors as necessary. The sauce should be neither overly sweet nor overly acidic. Add a little water if the sauce gets too thick. Stir in the chopped dill. It will keep, refrigerated, for 2 to 3 weeks. Sweet Mustard Sauce has 130.0 calories. Sweet Mustard Sauce has 14.0 grams of fat. Sweet Mustard Sauce has 88.0 grams of sodium. The ingredients of Sweet Mustard Sauce are 6 tablespoons sweet grainy mustard, or to taste, 2 to 3 tablespoons Dijon mustard, 1 tablespoon sugar, or to taste, 1 to 2 tablespoons vinegar, 1 cup vegetable oil, 2 to 3 tablespoons finely chopped fresh dill.Editor's note: The recipe and introductory text below are reprinted from Andreas Viestad's book Kitchen of Light: New Scandinavian Cooking.
