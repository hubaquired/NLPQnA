Heirloom Tomato and Burrata Cheese Salad recipe.  Cut tomatoes into wedges and place in large bowl. Sprinkle with fleur de sel and pepper. Crush oregano between palms to release flavor; add to tomatoes. Add 1/4 cup basil and olive oil and mix well. Let stand at room temperature at least 30 minutes and up to 1 hour, stirring occasionally. Place 1 burrata cheese round in center of each plate. Fan tomatoes around cheese, dividing equally. Drizzle with dressing from bowl. Garnish with additional basil leaves and serve. The ingredients of Heirloom Tomato and Burrata Cheese Salad are 4 large heirloom tomatoes (about 2 1/2 pounds) or 4 to 5 large plum tomatoes, Fleur de sel or coarse kosher salt, Freshly ground black pepper, 1 teaspoon dried oregano, 1/4 cup torn fresh basil leaves plus additional whole leaves for garnish, 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil, 4 (2.5-ounce) rounds burrata cheese.This summer, Marino grew about 85 tomato varieties in his garden; he uses his harvest in this caprese. Burrata cheese, a fresh mozzarella filled with cream and curds, is available at some supermarkets, Italian markets, and cheese stores. Fresh whole-milk mozzarella can be substituted.
