Summer Melon with Basil-Mint Granita recipe. Mix sugar, chopped mint, basil, and lime peel in medium bowl. Mash with wooden spoon until herbs turn dark green, about 3 minutes. Stir in lime juice and 1 1/4 cups water. Let stand 1 hour. Strain into large bowl, pressing on solids. Transfer 1/2 cup lime syrup to small pitcher; cover and refrigerate. Pour remaining lime syrup into 13x9x2-inch metal baking dish. Place dish in freezer. Stir syrup with fork every hour until frozen, about 4 hours. Can be made 1 day ahead. Cover and keep frozen.) Divide melon wedges among 8 shallow soup bowls. Drizzle melon with reserved lime syrup. Scrape granita into flakes with fork. Spoon generous 1/2 cup granita atop melon in each bowl. Sprinkle with sliced mint leaves, garnish with mint sprigs, and serve. Summer Melon with Basil-Mint Granita has 159.0 calories. Summer Melon with Basil-Mint Granita has 2.0 grams of protein. Summer Melon with Basil-Mint Granita has 33.0 grams of sodium. The ingredients of Summer Melon with Basil-Mint Granita are 3/4 cup plus 2 tablespoons sugar, 3/4 cup chopped fresh mint leaves, 3/4 cup chopped fresh basil leaves, 2 tablespoons grated lime peel, 1 1/2 cups fresh lime juice, 1 1/4 cups water, 3 small melons, halved lengthwise, seeded, each half cut into 8 wedges (about 3/4 inch wide), peeled, 2 tablespoons thinly sliced fresh mint leaves plus 8 fresh sprigs for garnish.Use any combination of ripe melons that you can find. Charentais (or Cavaillon), delicious French melons with orange flesh, are at farmers' markets now, while Galia (sweet and aromatic, similar to honeydew) and Sharlyn (a white-fleshed variety that tastes like a combination of honeydew and cantaloupe) are increasingly available at supermarkets.
