Pico de Gallo with Lemon Zest (Pico de gallo con limón amarillo) recipe.  Combine all the ingredients in a large bowl and stir thoroughly to distribute the ingredients well. Season to taste with more chile, lemon juice, or salt, if you want. I like to let the salsa sit for at least 30 minutes before serving. The ingredients of Pico de Gallo with Lemon Zest (Pico de gallo con limón amarillo) are 1 1/2 cups diced seeded tomatoes, 1/3 cup finely chopped red onion, Heaping 1/4 cup chopped cilantro, 2 teaspoons finely grated lemon zest, 2 tablespoons plus 1 teaspoon freshly squeezed lemon juice, or more to taste, 1 1/2 tablespoons finely chopped fresh serrano or jalapeño chiles (including seeds), or more to taste, 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt.Pico de gallo, also known as salsa Mexicana, has become a common sight on tables in the U.S. and it's easy to see why. The mixture of raw, chopped ingredients improves just about any meal with its lively acidity, lip-tingling heat, and crisp texture. This version is a riff on the classic that swaps lemon for lime to great effect. Whenever I take a bite I have a heretical thought: This is so delicious that maybe we Mexicans should use only lemons!.
