Classic Havana Frittata recipe. Deep-fry the plantain and potato separately in the canola oil until golden brown. In a large bowl, whisk together the eggs and heavy cream; add the paprika, salt, and pepper. Place the olive oil and butter in a 10-inch sauté pan with an ovenproof handle over medium-high heat. When the butter has melted, add the onion and sauté until brown. Add the plantain and potato to the egg mixture, then pour it into the pan. Cook for 5 minutes, scraping the pan regularly so the bottom sets. Place under a broiler until golden brown, 7 to 10 minutes. Serve with a simple mesclun salad optional). The ingredients of Classic Havana Frittata are 1 large ripe plantain, cut into 1/2-inch cubes, 1 medium baking potato, peeled and cut into 1/2-inch cubes, Canola oil, 8 large eggs, 1/2 cup heavy cream, 1 teaspoon sweet paprika, Salt and pepper to taste, 3 tablespoons olive oil, 2 tablespoons butter, 1 large yellow onion, diced.
