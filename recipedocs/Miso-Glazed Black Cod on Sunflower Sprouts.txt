Miso-Glazed Black Cod on Sunflower Sprouts recipe.  Whisk miso, 1/4 cup mirin, 2 tablespoons vinegar, ginger, and 2 teaspoons sesame oil in small bowl to blend. Place fish in 8x8x2-inch glass baking dish. Pour miso mixture over; turn to coat. Let stand 30 minutes at room temperature. Preheat broiler. Line baking sheet with foil; brush with 1 teaspoon oil. Place fish on foil; pour marinade over fish. Broil until fish is cooked through and brown in spots, about 6 minutes. Meanwhile, mix remaining 1 teaspoon mirin, 1 tablespoon vinegar, 1 teaspoon sesame oil, and 1/4 cup green onions in medium bowl. Add sunflower sprouts; toss to coat. Divide salad among 4 plates; top with fish and sprinkle with remaining green onions. Miso-Glazed Black Cod on Sunflower Sprouts has 228.0 calories. The ingredients of Miso-Glazed Black Cod on Sunflower Sprouts are 1/3 cup white miso (fermented soybean paste), 1/4 cup plus 1 teaspoon mirin (sweet Japanese rice wine), 3 tablespoons unseasoned rice vinegar, divided, 2 tablespoons minced peeled fresh ginger, 4 teaspoons toasted sesame oil (such as Asian), divided, 4 6-ounce skinless black cod fillets, 1/2 cup chopped green onions, divided, 5 ounces sunflower sprouts.Miso, a salty, earthy Japanese bean paste, is rich in vitamin B and protein. White miso, also known as shiro miso, has a more delicate flavor than darker miso pastes. Mirin is a sweet golden wine made from glutinous rice; it is essential to Japanese cooking.
