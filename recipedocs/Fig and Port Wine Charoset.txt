Fig and Port Wine Charoset recipe.  In small saucepan, combine port and figs. Set over high heat and bring to boil. Reduce heat and simmer, uncovered, until figs are softened and port is slightly reduced, about 15 minutes. Meanwhile, in large heavy-bottomed skillet over moderately high heat, heat oil. Add onions and cook, stirring often, until lightly caramelized, about 20 minutes. Remove from heat and cool slightly. In large bowl, combine fig/port mixture, onions, pine nuts, and pepper. Serve at room temperature. Fig and Port Wine Charoset has 125.0 calories. Fig and Port Wine Charoset has 1.0 grams of protein. Fig and Port Wine Charoset has 7.0 grams of fat. Fig and Port Wine Charoset has 6.0 grams of sodium. The ingredients of Fig and Port Wine Charoset are 1 cup ruby port, 1 cup black Mission figs, finely chopped, 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, 2 cups finely chopped Vidalia or other sweet onions, 1/3 cup pine nuts, lightly toasted (optional), 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper.In addition to its traditional role on the Seder plate, this charoset is delicious as a condiment for duck or roast beef.
