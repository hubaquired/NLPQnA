Wine-Braised Pork Loin recipe.  Combine first 5 ingredients in small bowl. Pat pork dry; rub with salt mixture. Cover and chill 6 to 24 hours. Preheat oven to 350°F. Wipe pork dry. Heat 2 tablespoons oil in heavy Dutch oven or casserole over high heat. Add pork; brown on all sides, about 10 minutes. Transfer to plate. Reduce heat to medium and add remaining 1 tablespoon oil to Dutch oven. Add onions and sauté until very tender, about 10 minutes. Add garlic and peppers and sauté until peppers begin to soften, about 5 minutes. Add wine, tomatoes, broth, bay leaves and marjoram. Add pork, fat side up, and drippings on plate. Bring to boil. Cover; bake until tender, 45 minutes. Transfer pork to platter and let stand 15 minutes. If necessary, boil sauce until reduced to 4 cups. Season with salt and pepper. Can be prepared 1 day ahead. Place half of sauce in baking dish. Top with pork slices, then remaining sauce. Cover and chill. Rewarm in covered dish in 350°F. oven until heated through, about 30 minutes.) Serve pork with sauce. The ingredients of Wine-Braised Pork Loin are 2 teaspoons salt, 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper, 1 bay leaf, crumbled, 1 garlic clove, minced, Pinch of ground allspice, 1 2-pound boned center-cut pork loin roast, rolled, tied, 3 tablespoons olive oil, 2 onions, chopped, 4 garlic cloves, chopped, 2 red bell peppers, cut lengthwise into strips, halved crosswise, 1 cup dry white wine, 1 cup canned crushed tomatoes with added puree, 1 cup canned beef broth, 2 bay leaves, 1 tablespoon dried marjoram, crumbled.
